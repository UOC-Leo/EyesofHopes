About Eyes of Hopes

Leo Club of University of Colombo

Eyes of Hopes is a compassionate initiative by the Leo Club of University of Colombo, dedicated to supporting the blind community and individuals with eye-related diseases while promoting awareness about eye care and vision protection.

Our Project Phases

Phase 1: Eye Donation and White Cane Distribution

Collecting eye donation pledges at temples to inspire vision restoration for corneal blindness, and distributing white canes to improve mobility and independence for the visually impaired.

Phase 2: Eye Screening & Spectacle Distribution

Conducting professional eye examinations and distributing spectacles to restore vision and improve quality of life for individuals with refractive errors.

Phase 3: Cataract Surgery Donation & Digital Awareness

Supporting cataract surgery initiatives and creating this interactive digital resource to promote eye care awareness and education for the community.

Vision

A world where everyone has access to quality eye care and no one is left in darkness due to preventable vision loss.

Mission

To promote eye health awareness, provide support to visually impaired individuals, and build a compassionate community dedicated to the gift of sight.

Goal

To reach thousands with eye health education, distribute aid to those in need, and inspire a culture of eye care and compassion.

Project Phases in Detail

Phase 1: Eye Donation and White Cane Distribution

Eyes of Hope was implemented to support the blind community and individuals suffering from eye related diseases, while creating awareness about eye care, vision impairment, and the importance of eye protection.

Session 1 - Eye Donation Pledge (January 3 & 11, 2026)

The first phase focused on promoting eye health awareness and encouraging eye donation. Eye donation pledge forms were collected to inspire individuals to donate their eyes after death and help restore vision for people with corneal blindness. These forms were collected at Gangaramaya Temple on the first Poya day of 2026 (3rd January) and later at Kelaniya Raja Maha Viharaya on 11th January 2026, where many devotees willingly pledged to give the gift of sight.

Session 2 - White Cane Distribution (January 17, 2026)

The second session focused on supporting visually impaired individuals by distributing white canes to improve their mobility, independence, and safety. This session was held on 17th January 2026 at the Sri Lanka Welfare Society of the Blind, Seeduwa, where participants had the opportunity to interact with the blind community and understand their experiences.

Impact: Eyes of Hope became more than a service project; it helped build empathy, human connection, and a deeper understanding of life. The initiative reminded us that even small acts of kindness can bring hope, compassion, and light to others.

Phase 2: Eye Screening & Spectacle Distribution

Eyes of Hope continued its mission aimed at supporting the blind community and individuals suffering from eye-related diseases while creating awareness about eye care and vision protection. The second phase of the project focused on providing direct medical support through eye checkups and spectacle distribution for individuals in need.

Eye Camp & Spectacle Distribution (January 15, 2026)

This phase was successfully held on 15th January 2026 from 9.00 a.m. onwards at the F.I. Fernando Memorial Hall, Young Buddhist Association, Beruwala. Many participants had been living with blurred vision, eye strain, and difficulty performing daily activities due to lack of access to proper eye care services.

Through professional eye examinations, individuals were able to understand their vision conditions and receive the necessary spectacles. The distribution of spectacles brought meaningful change, helping students read clearly, elders move with confidence, and workers carry out their daily tasks with ease.

Impact: The second phase of Eyes of Hope demonstrated how a simple act of care can restore vision, confidence, and independence. It strengthened our commitment to serving the community and spreading hope for a brighter future.

Eye Health Education

Learn about common eye conditions and how to maintain healthy vision

Myopia (Short Sight)

People with myopia can see nearby objects clearly, but distant objects appear blurry.

What is Myopia?

Myopia occurs when the eye is too long or the cornea is too curved, causing light to focus in front of the retina.

Prevention Tips:
  • Spend more time outdoors (at least 2 hours daily)
  • Maintain proper reading distance (25-30 cm)
  • Take regular screen breaks using the 20-20-20 rule
  • Ensure adequate lighting while reading
When to See a Doctor:

If you notice difficulty seeing the board in class or distant signs while driving.

Hyperopia (Long Sight)

People with hyperopia see distant objects clearly, but nearby objects appear blurry.

What is Hyperopia?

Hyperopia occurs when the eye is too short or the cornea is too flat, causing light to focus behind the retina.

Prevention Tips:
  • Regular eye checkups
  • Proper lighting for close work
  • Maintain good posture while reading
  • Avoid excessive strain during near work
When to See a Doctor:

If you experience headaches, eye strain, or difficulty focusing on nearby objects.

Astigmatism

Astigmatism causes blurred vision at all distances due to irregular cornea shape.

What is Astigmatism?

Astigmatism occurs when the cornea or lens has an irregular shape, causing blurred vision at all distances.

Prevention Tips:
  • Regular vision screening
  • Proper eye care and hygiene
  • Avoid eye injuries
  • Maintain overall eye health
When to See a Doctor:

If you experience blurred vision, eye strain, or headaches at any distance.

Cataract

A clouding of the eye lens that affects vision, usually in older adults.

What is Cataract?

Cataracts develop when the lens becomes cloudy, typically due to aging, but can also result from injury or disease.

Prevention Tips:
  • Wear UV-protective sunglasses
  • Maintain healthy diet rich in antioxidants
  • Don't smoke
  • Regular eye checkups after age 60
  • Protect eyes from injury
When to See a Doctor:

If you notice cloudy vision, difficulty seeing at night, or colors appearing faded.

Glaucoma

Increased eye pressure damages the optic nerve, potentially causing vision loss.

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma occurs when increased intraocular pressure damages the optic nerve, often without early symptoms.

Prevention Tips:
  • Regular eye pressure screening
  • Manage other health conditions (diabetes, hypertension)
  • Use prescribed eye drops regularly
  • Maintain healthy lifestyle
  • Protect eyes from trauma
When to See a Doctor:

Regular screening is essential as glaucoma often has no symptoms until vision loss occurs.

Digital Eye Strain

Discomfort from extended screen time, affecting students and office workers.

What is Digital Eye Strain?

Long periods of screen use can cause dry eyes, headaches, and blurred vision due to reduced blinking.

Prevention Tips:
  • Follow the 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds
  • Adjust screen brightness to match surroundings
  • Position screen at arm's length, slightly below eye level
  • Use artificial tears if eyes feel dry
  • Blink consciously while using screens
When to See a Doctor:

If symptoms persist even after implementing preventive measures.

Student Vision Care Guide

Special guide for university and school students to maintain healthy vision.

Why Eye Care Matters for Students

Students often overlook eye health due to academic pressure and excessive screen/study time, leading to vision problems.

Essential Care Tips:
  • Take breaks during study sessions
  • Maintain proper distance from books and screens (30-40 cm)
  • Ensure adequate lighting while studying
  • Get regular eye checkups (annually)
  • Spend time outdoors daily
  • Maintain healthy eating habits (vitamin A, C, E rich foods)
  • Manage stress through exercise and sleep

Eye Care for Elderly

Special considerations for maintaining vision as we age.

Age-Related Eye Changes

Aging naturally affects vision quality, but proper care can maintain independence and quality of life.

Care Recommendations:
  • Annual comprehensive eye exams
  • Wear adequate UV protection
  • Maintain healthy diet (leafy greens, fish, eggs)
  • Control blood pressure and diabetes
  • Exercise regularly
  • Ensure adequate lighting at home
  • Use appropriate eye aids if needed
  • Keep home free of hazards that could cause falls

UV Protection Tips

Protecting eyes from harmful UV radiation, especially for outdoor workers.

Why UV Protection Matters

UV radiation can cause cataracts, macular degeneration, and pterygium, especially in people who work outdoors.

Protection Tips:
  • Wear UV-blocking sunglasses (100% UVA/UVB protection)
  • Use wide-brimmed hats outdoors
  • Take regular breaks in shade
  • Apply UV-protective eye drops if needed
  • Consider blue light filtering lenses for screen use
  • Important for fishermen and outdoor workers: use protective eyewear at all times

Awareness Articles

Educational articles and insights about eye health and wellness

Student Health

Why University Students Ignore Eye Health

University life is demanding. Between attending lectures, studying late into the night, and spending countless hours on screens, it's easy for students to overlook one crucial aspect of their health: their eyes. Let's explore why this happens and what can be done about it.

  • Academic Pressure: Exams and assignments create stress that defers eye care.
  • Busy Lifestyle: Limited time means eye health takes a back seat.
  • No Immediate Symptoms: Eye problems often develop gradually, making them easy to ignore.
  • Solution: Regular eye checkups, screen breaks, and outdoor time are essential.
Screen Time

How Screen Time Affects Your Vision

In our digital age, screens are unavoidable. But excessive screen time can take a toll on your eyes, leading to digital eye strain and other vision problems. Understanding this impact is the first step toward protecting your vision.

  • Digital Eye Strain: Caused by reduced blinking and focusing on screens.
  • Symptoms: Dry eyes, headaches, blurred vision, and neck pain.
  • Long-term Effects: Prolonged screen exposure may accelerate myopia progression in children.
  • Prevention: Take regular breaks using the 20-20-20 rule.
Child Health

Signs Your Child Needs Glasses

Early detection of vision problems in children is crucial for their development and learning. Here are the warning signs that your child might need glasses or a comprehensive eye examination.

  • Performance: Poor school performance, especially when previously doing well.
  • Physical Signs: Squinting, tilting head, or sitting too close to screens.
  • Behavior: Avoiding reading, eye discomfort, or frequent headaches.
  • Action: Schedule an eye exam with a professional if you notice these signs.
Preventive Care

Importance of Regular Eye Checkups

Many eye diseases develop without noticeable symptoms. Regular eye checkups can detect problems early when they're most treatable. Don't wait until your vision is affected to get your eyes examined.

  • Detection: Early detection of cataracts, glaucoma, and other diseases.
  • Health Indicator: Eyes can reveal signs of diabetes, hypertension, and other conditions.
  • Prevention: Preventive care preserves vision and prevents complications.
  • Recommendation: Annual checkups for healthy adults; more frequent for those with risk factors.
Nutrition

Foods That Promote Healthy Vision

What you eat directly affects your eye health. Nutrients like lutein, zeaxanthin, vitamins C and E, and omega-3 fatty acids are essential for maintaining clear vision and preventing age-related eye diseases.

  • Leafy Greens: Spinach, kale - rich in lutein.
  • Fish: Salmon, mackerel - excellent source of omega-3.
  • Eggs: Contain lutein and zeaxanthin.
  • Citrus: Oranges, lemons - high in vitamin C.
  • Nuts: Almonds - contain vitamin E.
Safety Tips

Eye Safety for Fishermen & Outdoor Workers

People who work outdoors, especially fishermen, face unique eye health challenges due to UV exposure, salt spray, and wind. Proper protection is essential for maintaining long-term vision health.

  • UV Protection: Always wear 100% UVA/UVB blocking sunglasses.
  • Protective Eyewear: Use goggles or face shields for additional protection.
  • Rest & Care: Take regular breaks and rinse eyes with fresh water.
  • Regular Checkups: More frequent eye exams due to increased risk.
  • Prevention: Wide-brimmed hats and shade when possible.
